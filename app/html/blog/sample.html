3. Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs)

Learning Objective: Identify, prioritize, and monitor NFRs throughout the project lifecycle.

a. Defining NFRs

  • Topics:
    • Types: Performance, security, availability, scalability.
    • Metrics: SLA, SLO, SLI, RTO/RPO.
  • Resources:
    • Book: Designing Data-Intensive Applications by Martin Kleppmann.
    • NFR checklist templates.
  • Activities:
    • Define SLOs for an e-commerce platform.
    • Conduct a risk assessment for a healthcare app.

b. Managing NFRs

  • Topics:
    • Trade-off analysis (e.g., security vs.Â usability).

4. Presenting Architecture

Learning Objective: Tailor presentations for executives, clients, and technical reviewers.

a. Presenting to Executives

  • Topics:
    • Focus on ROI, risk mitigation, strategic alignment.
    • Simplify jargon; use analogies and visuals.
  • Resources:
    • TED Talk: How to Present to Senior Executives by Nancy Duarte.
    • Template: Executive summary one-pager.
  • Activities:
    • Pitch a cloud migration to a mock CFO (focus on cost savings).

b. Presenting to Clients

  • Topics:
    • Align with client pain points; use storytelling.
    • Balance technical depth with business value.
  • Resources:
    • Book: Articulating Design Decisions by Tom Greever.
  • Activities:
    • Present a solution to a client persona (e.g., retail CEO worried about scalability).

Timeline & Certification Path

  • Month 1: Complete AWS/Azure Solutions Architect Associate cert + TOGAF Foundation.
  • Month 2: Build a portfolio with 2-3 documented case studies.
  • Month 3: Develop an NFR checklist and present to a mock ARB.
  • Month 4: Deliver executive/client presentations for feedback.

Final Project: Design and document a full-stack architecture for a scalable SaaS product, including NFRs and executive/client/ARB presentations.
